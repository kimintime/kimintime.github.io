My Philosophy on rules and why you don't need that many

Rules were made to be broken. How many times have you heard you should never write in a certain voice, or that there's no such thing as filler, or that you should never write more than one book at a time? The list goes on and on, and I can promise you that for every Rule of Writing, there are countless examples of landmark books that broke those rules.

The editing process

  1. Just keep writing
  2. Put it away for a while, get some distance
  3. When you pick it up again, text-to-speech or reading out loud helps!
  4. Rewrite and write again
  5. From here it's personal preference, whether it's critique partners, or alpha and beta readers, but it's time for your baby to see the light of day.
  6. After further drafting, It's time for a developmental editor to have a look.
What does a developmental editor do?
  • Catches plotholes
  • Finds and develops the themes in the book
  • Helps the author tell the story they intended to tell, in their voice
  • Maintains the timing and flow
  • Develops story and character archs
  • Is often a first line of defense in the line editing process, but is NOT a line editor
  • Is often the first line of defense in the proofreading process, but is NOT a proofreader
